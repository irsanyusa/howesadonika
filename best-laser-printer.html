What to consider when choosing a laser printer

Volume

Whether you need to print 20 pages per day or 2,000, you’ll want to ensure your laser printer has the capacity to do that. First off, take into consideration the maximum pages per minute. Most printers can reach anywhere between 20 to 35 pages per minute which makes it great for high-volume needs whether at a school or an office. Next, think about the paper tray. How many pieces of paper can it hold at once? If the answer is 250 or more, then it’s a great option since it won’t need as many refills.

Color

Laser printers are available in both monochrome or color. Monochrome printers are best suited for your standard documents—think tax forms, waivers, letters, etc. On the other hand, color printers are better for basic images and graphics that can be accentuated by vibrant hues. Keep in mind that colored ink is usually more expensive so you’ll want to factor that into your budget.

Features

Printers have a plethora of features depending on the brand and model. For example, on some printers you’ll notice touch-screen interfaces and voice command capabilities which is suitable for those tech nerds looking for added ease and convenience. Other printers are a bit more basic with standard buttons and copy/print modes. In general, most laser printers these days are made to hold a large volume of paper, print at a quick speed, and copy, fax, and scan all with one device.

Ways to maintain a laser printer

After investing money in a laser printer, you’ll want to maintain it for optimal efficiency and longevity. On the surface, wipe it down with a no-lint towel and some rubbing alcohol to get rid of any dirt or dust that might have accumulated.

In terms of the printer itself, try not to leave it plugged in. When you’re not using it, unplug it from the wall, but only after powering it down first. This process will allow for the cartridges to stay pigmented and last longer. If you notice your cartridges are running low, then you’ll want to replace them quickly. Don’t wait until the last minute as this will add extra strain onto your printer and its parts, which can damage it quicker.

What kind of connectivity to look for in a laser printer

These days, you’ll want to find a laser printer that has wireless connectivity so you can print from places both far and near. Some laser printers have the ability to connect with mobile phones via an app so you don’t even have to go through the hassle of using a computer. Some models allow for USB connectivity so if that’s preferred, then you’ll have another way to print. You’ll want to check with each printer model to see what type of connectivity options they offer and pick one that best suits your needs.

Types of laser printers

There are two different types of laser printers: monochrome and color. “Monochrome laser printers are best suited for tasks like printing reports, tax documents, and other day to day office needs,” says Nick Lucido, Communications Specialist at HP. “Color laser printers are optimal for those who need to create vibrant documents like marketing outlines and sales tools.”

Additionally, there are multifunction laser printers that help tackle a range of office tasks from printing and copying to scanning and faxing, with different versions offering different combinations of features.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

Which brand is best for laser printers?

There is no one specific brand that is the best for laser printers, however, you’ll want to find one that best suits your needs. For example, HP printers are designed for individuals looking for a higher-tech printing experience with options like voice control and touch-screen displays. On the other hand, Brother and Canon boast more intuitive designs and have faster print speeds which is best suited for busy environments like offices or libraries.

What is the disadvantage of a laser printer?

While laser printers are great for large volume printing needs, Lucido notes they’re not designed for producing high-quality, gallery-worthy photos or color prints. “Photographers, designers, and others who require precise color printing should consider an inkjet printer,” he adds.

Are laser printers worth buying?

Laser printers are a worthwhile alternative to standard inkjet printers and provide sustainability and cost-saving benefits. With minimal ink cartridge replacement needs and economical toner powder, total cost of ownership for lasers is usually lower than owning an inkjet. “Additionally, laser printers typically offer significantly faster printing speeds compared to inkjets, making them particularly appealing for those with high-volume printing requirements, saving money and time around big printing jobs,” says Lucido.

What is the average lifespan of a laser printer?

The average lifespan of a laser printer varies based on its design and usage. Lucido says, “Each printer is tailored to a specific customer segment with recommended monthly page volumes ensuring efficient operation and longevity.” That said, many laser printers generally last between three to five years at most.

How to dispose of your old printer?

Many brands have programs where you can recycle an old printer. For example, HP’s Planet Partners program allows printer owners to recycle their devices through a local retailer or HP recycling partner in an effort to prevent sending printers and cartridges to landfills.

Additionally, many cities have special instructions on how to properly dispose of electronic waste. You’ll want to check with your local sanitation website for more information.
