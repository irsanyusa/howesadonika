Like finding the best swimsuit, shopping for intimate apparel should be fun. Here are some ways to make sure you find the best piece for your body and your budget. Types of lingerie you need or want

Understand all the different types of lingerie available. Start with a set of basic items (bras, panties, slips, or bodysuits), and then build your wardrobe according to season and occasion.

Take your measurements

Make sure you (or better yet, a professional fitter) take your measurements, especially bust. Write down all your sizes before you purchase items online or in brick-or-mortar stores. Just like clothing, lingerie may differ in size depending on the designer.

Make sure you are comfortable

Gone are the days (well, we hope at least) of choosing fashion over comfort. With so many great lingerie designers out there, you can find well-fitting, sexy garments that are a pleasure to wear.

Choose quality if you can afford to

Well-made lingerie in breathable fabrics and materials is always a better choice, even if it comes at a higher price point. Well-made lingerie will last much longer than cheap garments.

What are the different types of lingerie?

There are many different types of lingerie. The main pieces of a lingerie wardrobe usually include but are not limited to panties (thongs, for example), bras, bodysuits, slips, garters and suspenders, sheer stockings, camisoles, and bralettes.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

How should I store my lingerie?

Hang up slips and gowns using padded hangers or ones with notches to keep slinky straps from slipping off. Consider keeping stockings, garters, and belts together (if they are sets), and consider using drawer dividers to house bras and underwear separately. Some people like to store special undergarments in mesh or other small bags.

How should I wash my lingerie?

Most lingerie should be hand-washed with gentle detergent, especially if it’s made from silk, satin, or lace. Let garments soak for up to 30 minutes and then rinse items thoroughly.

Many everyday bras and panties may be machine washable, preferably in a mesh laundry bag on delicate, and then (ideally) hang items dry. Always read the directions on the labels before tossing anything into a washing machine or dryer.

What is the difference between lingerie and underwear?

Underwear usually refers to the more functional undergarments like bras and bikini briefs and lingerie is usually casts a wider net to include not only bras and panties but also chemises, slips, stockings and suspenders, and pieces created with a specific aesthetic (often sexy, romantic or flirty).
