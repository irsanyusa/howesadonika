Health benefits of tea | Pulse Nigeria

From weight management to stress relief, different types of tea contribute to your overall well-being.

1. Green tea

ADVERTISEMENT

Green tea is minimally processed, preserving its high concentration of antioxidants called catechins. These antioxidants are believed to offer a range of benefits, including:

  • Weight management: Studies suggest that green tea may help boost metabolism and promote fat burning.
  • Cognitive function: Green tea may improve alertness and focus due to its caffeine content and L-theanine, an amino acid known for promoting relaxation.
  • Heart health: Green tea's antioxidants may contribute to lower LDL (low-density lipoprotein) cholesterol, sometimes called “bad” cholesterol levels and improve blood vessel health.

Green tea is naturally delicate and slightly grassy in flavour. Steep it in hot water for 2-3 minutes for a refreshing and invigorating brew.

2. Black tea

ADVERTISEMENT

Black tea undergoes full oxidation, resulting in a stronger flavour and higher caffeine content compared to green tea. Black tea offers potential benefits like:

  • Improved alertness: The caffeine in black tea can help you feel more awake and focused.
  • Hydration: While some believe tea dehydrates you, the truth is, it contributes to your daily fluid intake. Enjoy black tea alongside water for optimal hydration.
  • Gut health: Early research suggests black tea may promote the growth of beneficial gut bacteria.

Black tea is known for its flavour. To enjoy, steep it in hot water for 3-5 minutes for a classic cup of black tea.

3. Oolong tea

ADVERTISEMENT

Oolong tea falls between green and black tea in terms of processing. It offers a spectrum of flavours depending on the variety and can provide benefits like:

  • Weight management: Oolong tea may help regulate blood sugar levels, potentially aiding in weight management.
  • Skin health: Some studies suggest oolong tea may have anti-inflammatory properties that could benefit skin health.

The ideal steeping temperature and time for oolong tea vary depending on the specific type. Generally, use hot water and steep for 2-5 minutes.

4. Herbal teas

ADVERTISEMENT

Herbal teas offer a variety of health benefits and are a great caffeine-free choice.

Here are some popular herbal teas and their potential perks:

  • Peppermint tea: Aid digestion and soothe an upset stomach.
  • Chamomile tea: Known for its calming properties, chamomile tea can promote relaxation and better sleep.
  • Ginger tea: May help reduce nausea and inflammation.

Steeping times for herbal teas can vary. Generally, use hot water and steep for 5-10 minutes.

ADVERTISEMENT

5. White tea

White tea is the least processed tea, made from young, unopened buds and leaves. This minimal processing results in a delicate flavour and a high concentration of antioxidants. Here are some potential benefits of white tea:

  • Skin health: White tea's antioxidants may help protect your skin from sun damage and promote overall skin health.
  • Immune system support: Early research suggests white tea may boost the immune system and help fight off infections.
  • Dental health: White tea may have antibacterial properties that can help reduce plaque formation and promote oral health.

Due to its delicate nature, white tea requires cooler water and a shorter steeping time of 1-2 minutes.

A note on weight management

While tea may offer some weight management benefits, it's important to understand that it's not a magic bullet. Tea, combined with a healthy diet and regular exercise, can be a helpful tool for weight management, but it shouldn't be the sole strategy.

Consult your doctor before making any significant changes to your diet or if you have any underlying health conditions, enjoy tea in moderation, especially those containing caffeine, and focus on a balanced and sustainable approach to weight management.
