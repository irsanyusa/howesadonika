How to Watch the 2024 BAFTA TV Awards Online

The 2024 BAFTA TV Awards are here. The ceremony will be broadcast on BBC One and stream on BBC iPlayer on Sunday night in the U.K., starting at 7 p.m. BST. Viewers in the U.S., Canada, Australia and more can watch on BritBox, starting at 2 p.m. ET. Winners will also be announced on BAFTA’s social media channels and website.

Leading this year’s ceremony with the most nominations is Netflix’s final season of “The Crown.” Four of the actors in the series picked up nominations including Dominic West (Prince Charles), Elizabeth Debicki (Princess Diana), Salim Daw (Mohamed Al-Fayed) and Lesley Manville (Princess Margaret). “The Crown” also scored nominations in make up, costume design and sound.

Meanwhile, “Black Mirror” received seven nominations, while “Slow Horses,” “Happy Valley” and “The Sixth Commandment” all have six. “Succession,” “The Last of Us” and “The Long Shadow” all earned five.

The BBC had a strong year, earning 65 nominations, while Netflix followed with 35. Steaming platforms also earned noms with Apple TV+’s Slow Horses and Disney+’s “The Bear” and “Extraordinary.”

This year’s snubs include Imelda Staunton who didn’t get nominated for her leading actress last season in “The Crown” as Queen Elizabeth II. Gary Oldman also didn’t get recognized this year for his work in “Slow Horses.”

BAFTA’s president Prince William, along with Kate Middleton will not be in attendance at this Sunday’s ceremony. However, Deadline reported that a video message of him will play during the program for the four actors nominated for “The Crown.”

Rob Beckett and Romesh Ranganathan are this year’s hosts. The ceremony will take place at Southbank Centre’s Royal Festival Hall in London.
